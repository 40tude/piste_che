Good question — both exist, but they’re **not used the same way**.

### 🎿 In natural English (especially American English)

👉 People almost always say **“slope”** or **“run”**

* **slope** → general, very common
  *“Let’s hit the slopes”*
* **run** → more precise (a specific trail)
  *“That was a great run”*

---

### 🇫🇷 “Piste” in English

* The word **“piste”** *does exist*, but:

  * it’s **borrowed from French**
  * sounds **more European / technical / ski-resort jargon**
  * less common in everyday American usage

You’ll see it in:

* resort maps
* British English sometimes
* official signage

---

### 🧠 Practical advice for your app

* If you want it to feel **natural / accessible** → use:
  👉 **run** (best choice overall)
  👉 or **slope**

* If you want a **“ski culture / Alps vibe”**:
  👉 **piste** can actually be cool stylistically

---

### ✅ My recommendation

For your project:

* UI / UX:
  👉 **run** (clear + natural)

* Branding / name:
  👉 **piste** can be fun and distinctive
  (e.g. *PistePlease* works better than *RunPlease*)
